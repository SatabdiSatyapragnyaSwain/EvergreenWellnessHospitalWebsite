General & Laparoscopic

General Surgery

General surgery refers to a broad field of surgery that involves the treatment of various conditions affecting the abdomen, digestive tract, skin, soft tissues, and other areas of the body. General surgeons perform a wide range of procedures, such as appendectomies (removal of the appendix), hernia repairs, gallbladder removals, and more.

Laparoscopic Surgery

Laparoscopic surgery, also known as minimally invasive surgery, is a modern surgical technique where operations are performed through small incisions. A camera (laparoscope) is inserted through one of the incisions to guide the surgeon, allowing them to view the surgical site on a screen. Laparoscopic surgery offers several benefits, including smaller scars, reduced risk of infection, shorter recovery times, and less postoperative pain compared to traditional open surgery.
